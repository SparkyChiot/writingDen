
Love is a thing Kenma Kozume doesn’t need.

At least that’s what he believes.

He understands the need for platonic love, he found that with his friends and family throughout the years—It’s romantic love he doesn’t need.

This seems especially apparent when Kuroo decides to drag him to a mall to “find him a girl.”

“Kuro, it isn’t the 2000s, nobody hangs out at the mall anymore.” Kenma complained, eyes fixated on the tiled floor. Kuroo had forbidden him from taking his PSP on their outing, arguing that he wouldn’t need it if he was taking their task seriously. “Would you rather me have taken you to a bar?” Kuroo replied with a sarcastic smile, and Kenma sighed, surveying his surroundings. The scene before them looked straight out of a magazine, various boutiques and restaurants lining the walls surrounding a large plaza. A fountain with floral accents sat in the center, light shining down through the glass ceiling a few floors above; the large atrium would look idyllic if it weren’t for the jarring lack of occupants.

”Look at that girl over there! She’s cute,” the shorter boy looked over to the girl his friend had mentioned. He hadn’t noticed her before, she sat on a bench closer to the left wing of the building, where the light didn’t quite reach. Her chin-length, dark hair fell messily around her sharp face, and her shirt clung tightly to her curves. Objectively, she wasn’t unattractive, he just didn’t see why that meant he should like her, he didn’t know anything about her. “I guess she’s cute,” Kenma started, though he didn’t quite understand how that was supposed to make him feel. When he looked at her, there weren't any sparks, no butterflies in his stomach, she was just a girl who happened to be in his vicinity.

Kuroo just grinned, already starting to walk toward the girl. “Well come on, let’s introduce ourselves!” Kenma suppressed a groan and trailed behind his friend. His mind filled with a cacophony of thoughts, what was he missing? What did Kuroo see in this girl that he didn’t? Why were they even approaching her anyways? 

A noise cut through the buzzing in his head, a soft voice. “Oh hi! I’m Yuka, what’s your friend’s name?” He suddenly felt their eyes on him as he looked up, quickly dipping his head so his hair hid him from their gaze. 

“Kozume. Kozume Kenma,” he responded quietly, still avoiding direct eye contact.

“Well, I like your hair, Kozume-san, it’s unique!” Kuroo gave him a smug grin. Kenma knew she was probably just being nice, there’s not really any reason for him to believe she was interested in him. Still, he appreciated the compliment, even if it wasn’t exactly what he was going for.

“Thank you,” he spoke softly, ”you can just call me Kenma.”
